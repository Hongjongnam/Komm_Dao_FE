Komm DAO Charter

and Operating Agreement based on The Origami Framework.

Last Revised Date: Jan 1, 2024

  1. About Komm DAO

  1. Mission

  2. To empower the Web3 ecosystem through Kommunity-driven investment and Kollective excellence

  3. Vision

  4. Komm DAO positions itself to be the leading K-ommunity and K-ollective of gurus in the Web3 space, members offering unique talent and insights and Web3 companies excelling in Korea. Our members bring their passion and skills, exchange ideas, and execute investments, in turn, democratizing access to private markets. We plan to establish a DAO K-ommittee, specialized in localization and penetration capabilities in the Korean market, to incubate and accelerate more global Web3 startups. While currently in the experimental stage, Komm DAO will progressively decentralize its operations and governance ownership.

  1. Member Privileges and Obligations

  1. Like in a cooperative, members have the privilege to enjoy rewards. In return, they have shared responsibilities toward achieving a common goal. Komm DAO requires high member participation to be successful; it is not for spectators, therefore we as Members:
  1. Participate in the discussion, creation, voting and documentation of proposals.
  2. Make friction, tension and inefficiencies known to all DAO members, so that changes, improvements and learning can take place.
  3. Monitor and record progress, and use it for the creation of metrics to measure DAO health.

  1. Membership Requirements

  1. By joining the DAO, you agree to:
  1. Abide by this Charter,
  2. Share the values of the Code of Ethics,
  3. Act in accordance with the Code of Conduct,
  4. Participate in the official communications channels on a regular basis.
  5. Act in the best interest of the DAO and its members.

  1. Membership

  1. Komm DAO is open to:

  1. Investors with specialized knowledge in Web3 and a risk-tolerance for private investments
  2. Researchers with knowledge in the latest technological advancements and market trends
  3. Marketers and branding experts who play a crucial role in promoting the DAO's mission and vision to a wider audience, as well as in creating and implementing effective branding strategies for portfolio companies associated with the DAO
  4. Legal and accounting practitioners with with experiences in fund investments and operations
  5. Developers with experience in web3 technologies and smart contract development
  6. Designers with skills in user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) design to create user-friendly applications and interfaces for the decentralized web
  7. Headhunters and talent scouts who can identify and recruit top talent to join the DAO's growing community and portfolio companies
  8. And others as authorized by the DAO.
  9. Founders and staff of Origami, Inc. who are providing services to the DAO.
  1. Komm DAO operations are coordinated by teams of elected members who serve on the Governance, Treasury, and Programs Committees that serve the DAO.
  2. A Member is required to mint a DAO Membership NFT that serves as proof of DAO membership.
  1. The DAO may gate access to DAO spaces and services — physical and digital — using the DAO Membership NFT and/or governance tokens.
  1. The implementation of such token-gating is the responsibility of the Programs Committee.
  1. The wallet containing the DAO Membership NFT is eligible to vote using the governance tokens in the same wallet.
  2. An EVM-compatible wallet with the same address across different chains is considered the same wallet as long as the Member has control over the wallets.
  3. The DAO Membership NFT may not be transferred without Program Committee authorization, unless allowed by Proposal.

  1. DAO Health

  1. Komm DAO will measure its health in the following ways:
  1. Members are participating.
  2. Membership is growing.
  3. The DAO is working to fulfill its Mission and Vision.
  4. Startups are supported by the DAO and its members.
  5. The DAO financially supports its own operations.
  6. Documents and code are created, amended, and published as lessons are learned.

  1. Removal of Members

  1. Members may be removed from membership in the DAO by Governance Committee vote for violating this Charter, the Code of Conduct or Code of Ethics.
  1. Removal of membership is defined as the exclusion of the member from DAO spaces, processes, and tools.
  2. If the removed member has been asked to give up their DAO tokens but refuses to do so, the DAO may deploy software updates to exclude that Member.
  3. Removed Members are no longer Members in Good Standing.
  1. The DAO shall burn or invalidate the DAO Membership NFT of a Removed Member.
  1. Proposal for Repeal of Removal
  1. If a member is removed, there is a 7-day window where the Removed Member may submit a Proposal for Repeal of Removal.
  2. The Proposal must be sponsored by three (3) or more members in good standing to be put up for DAO-wide vote.
  3. The vote on this Proposal will follow the normal proposal DAO-wide voting process.

  1. Resolving Conflicts

  1. Conflicts and disagreements are normal and part of any human endeavor. Members are expected to apply our values in creating and modifying the instruments for conflict resolution. See Resolving Conflict.
  2. Members of the Permanent Committees may ask for clarification regarding this Charter from the founders of Origami, Inc.
  1. The DAO is free to accept or ignore such clarifications.

  1. Taxes and Legal Compliance

  1. Taxes and legal compliance are a Member’s own responsibility.
  2. Nothing published or claimed by the DAO or its members constitutes legal or tax advice.

  1. No Partnership; Limitation on Liability

  1. Each Member agrees that the intent underlying the DAO’s operations is to support future Members and not to carry on a business for profit. For the avoidance of doubt, the DAO is not a partnership, and each Member hereby represents that they are not becoming a Member with the intent to form a partnership or to join a partnership as a partner. Members further understand and acknowledge that they do not individually have managerial control or authority over the day-to-day operations or the conducting of the business of the DAO.
  2. Each Member further agrees and acknowledges that no individual Member shall be liable for the actions of another Member, and that any and all liabilities arising from actions of the DAO shall be attributed solely to the DAO and not to its individual Members, regardless of whether such liability arose in the ordinary course of the DAO’s business operations. In the event any Member incurs a liability in connection with the activities or business operations of the DAO, no other Member shall be jointly or severally liable by virtue of their status as a Member. No individual Member shall have the authority to bind the DAO absent approval by a vote of DAO Members, and each Member hereby covenants not to hold themselves out as having any such authority absent approval by a vote of DAO Members.
  3. Further, each Member agrees and acknowledges that Members do not owe fiduciary duties to one another or the DAO itself, and that any such fiduciary duties are affirmatively eliminated to the extent any existed to the maximum extent permitted by law.
  4. In addition, each Member acknowledges and agrees that despite the above, a regulatory authority or governmental body may find that the DAO is in fact a partnership if it finds that the DAO is an association of two or more persons formed to carry on a business purpose for profit. Each Member further acknowledges and agrees that, despite the above, if a regulatory or governmental body finds that the DAO is a partnership, it may further find that each member is a partner in such partnership if it finds that members’ governance rights in the DAO are tantamount to managerial control of the DAO.

  1. DAO Organization and Operations and Governance

  1. DAOs are Decentralized and Member-governed

  1. Komm DAO builds on shared interests managed through open and secure software that will help in the DAO’s governance. As we experiment with day-to-day operations, we should always consider:
  1. Is our operation following the mission and vision of Komm DAO?
  2. Is the solution repeatable and should it be coded into our processes, smart contracts, and/or software?

  1. Effective Date

  1. The Effective Date is the date of the genesis distribution of the first Membership NFT. 

  1. Seasons

  1. Seasons are a defined time period of DAO operations.
  2. There are 2 Seasons (6 months each) per approximately 365 days.
  3. Seasons must be consecutive without a gap in dates unless otherwise decided by the Governance Committee.
  4. Season 0 is from the Effective Date of this Charter until June 30, 2023.
  1. The following Season starts at midnight the day after the end of the previous Season.
  2. The Governance Committee sets the Season dates not prescribed in this Charter.

  1. Branding

  1. The DAO shall use the “Korean Guru” emoji {🦸🏻‍♂} as its branded emoji. “🦸🏻‍♂ DAO”, or “Komm DAO”, as typeset are acceptable. Use of the DAO branding requires the DAO’s approval.
  1. For example, you cannot use the DAO’s branding in a way that implies that the DAO supports another project without the approval of the DAO or approval from a Permanent Committee.

  1. Committees, Sub-Committees, and Teams

  1. Permanent Committees, also known as Committees
  1. To aid the operations of the DAO and to safeguard its integrity and assets, the DAO will establish a minimum of three (3) Permanent Committees.
  2. The three Permanent Committees are:
  1. Governance Committee
  2. Programs Committee
  3. Treasury Committee
  1. Each Committee will be staffed with a minimum of three (3) Members.
  1. To ensure a separation of powers, no Member may serve on more than one committee in the same any Season.term.
  1. Each Committee is responsible for proposing a budget of tokens for their Committee for the Season.
  1. This budget request may be sent to the Treasury Committee to create a Proposal.
  2. Each Committee may create their own Proposal for Season funding in lieu of being a part of the seasonal Treasury budget process.
  1. Every Sub-Committee and Team must report to a Permanent Committee to ensure accountability.
  2. A committee member may serve under a subcommittee of the same committee, but cannot serve in the subcommittee of another committee.
  3. A term is a Season or the remainder of a Season.
  4. Replacement of Committee Members
  1. If one or more members of a Permanent Committee resigns or is removed for any reason, the remaining Committee member or members may chose to:
  1. Appoint a replacement member to fill the remainder of the term, or
  2. Hold an election to fill that seat for the remainder of the term.
  1. The DAO, by Proposal, may vote to leave the seat empty for the remainder of the term.
  1. Each Committee is responsible for proposing a budget for the Season and distributing it to the Sub-Committees and Teams that report to it.
  2. Sub-Committees
  1. All Permanent Committees, at their discretion, may form or disband a Sub-Committee.
  1. All Sub-Committees must report to a Committee for oversight.
  2. A Sub-Committee may also be created by Proposal, but it must report to a Committee for oversight.
  1. For a Sub-Committee created by Proposal, a Committee overseeing such a Sub-Committee may disband it only after a minimum of one (1) Season’s equivalent of days have elapsed since the passing of such Proposal.
  1. A Permanent Committee may delegate its duties to its Sub-Committees.
  1. A Sub-Committee is made up of one or more Members of the DAO.
  1. All positions in Sub-Committees are appointed by the Committee that oversees it.
  2. Any Sub-Committee member may be removed by the overseeing Committee.
  1. A Sub-Committee may not have a separate multisig for its operations. If a Sub-Committee requires their own multisig, it must be converted to a Team.
  1. Teams (also known as Kohorts)
  1. Any group of members may form a Team at any time.
  1. A Team must choose a Permanent Committee to report to.
  2. A Team must have its own multisig with at least 2 signer threshold of 3 signatories.
  3. If the Team does not have enough signatories, they may recruit DAO members not on the Team as signatories.
  4. A member may belong to many Teams or Sub-Committees.
  5. A member may belong to a Committee and Teams.
  6. Unlike Permanent Committees, Teams are impermanent.
  1. Funding a Team requires either:
  1. A Proposal to pass, or
  2. A Committee to allocate a portion of the Committee’s own budget.
  1. Disbanding any team can be done by the Team, or by DAO-wide vote.
  1. A Committee may not force a Team to disband.
  2. However, funding for the Team is at the discretion of the DAO by Proposal and/or Committee.
  1. Teams are responsible for setting their own rules and processes for decision-making or compensation of Team members.
  2. Teams are free to decide their own organizational structure, membership requirements, or removal process.
  1. Teams may include non-members.
  1. A Team may be also called a Sub-DAO if the team issues their own governance tokens and adapts a form of this Charter.

  1. Governance Committee

  1. The Governance Committee:
  1. Builds trust between its members and between the DAO and other entities.
  2. Ensures the integrity of the Charter, proposals process, voting, and discussion.
  3. Resolves any conflict of interest, rules on ethical questions, and disagreements.
  4. Is responsible for the structure and maintenance of the DAOs legal entities.
  5. Conducts elections for all Permanent Committee seats each Season.
  6. Keeps this Charter up-to-date when proposals require the amendment of this Charter.
  7. All changes to this Charter must be passed through the Proposal process.

  1. Treasury Committee

  1. The Treasury Committee:
  1. Will seek to safeguards the DAO’s assets
  2. Distributes tokens according to the will of the DAO membership through the, proposal processes,
  3. Manages the DAO’s finances,
  1. The Treasury Committee members are required to be on the key DAO multisig wallet(s).
  2. Prior to the end of the current season, the The Treasury Committee is responsible for proposing a budget — the allocation of a pool of tokens to all Permanent Committees — for the current season.and/or subsequent seasons.
  1. It is the Treasury Committee’s responsibility to propose the minting of new DAO tokens beyond the current limit of 1 billion tokens.

  1. Programs Committee

  1. The Programs Committee:
  1. Oversees the creation and maintenance of software, tools, and documents.
  2. Is responsible for any projects that fall outside the responsibilities of the Governance and Treasury Committees.
  3. Oversees the growth of the DAO’s membership and capabilities through direct development of programs and partnerships.
  4. Is responsible for maintaining the DAOs membership information, managing use of the DAO branding, and processes and safeguarding information.

  1. Election of the Committee Members

  1. Upon the Effective Date of the Charter, the Members of each Committee will be voted upon by the membership.
  2. While there is no current limit to the term of any member on a Committee or Sub-Committee, the DAO may set a term limit on any position in the DAO by proposal.
  3. Elections should be concluded prior to the start of the Season in which the results will come into effect.
  1. If elections are not concluded, the end of the current Season is automatically extended until concluded.

  1. Impeachment of a Committee Member

  1. To remove a Permanent Committee member from their Committee role, 50% or more of all the Permanent Committees members must agree to the removal.
  1. The Permanent Committee member being considered for removal cannot vote in this decision.
  2. Impeachment from a Permanent Committee does not remove the member from the DAO.

  1. Power to Form Teams and Subcommittees

  1. Any permanent committee may form subcommittees to distribute its committee work. It may appoint and remove other DAO members to these subcommittees as needed.
  2. Teams are ad-hoc groups of DAO members who are responsible for executing a proposal for a specific period of time.
  1. Teams of members may be allocated a budget of tokens from the DAO treasury in pursuit of DAO objectives.
  2. Teams are impermanent and their funding requires approval from one or more Committees or the passing of a Proposal.

  1. Proposals and Quorum

  1. The following actions require the passing of a Proposal using the DAO’s governance tokens:
  1. Elections of Permanent Committee members.
  2. Amendments to the Charter.
  3. Allocation of tokens from the Treasury.
  4. Committing the DAO to specific objectives.
  1. All other decisions are carried out by the respective Permanent Committees or by the responsible Teams.

  2. Proposals

  1. All Proposals will conform to the default time period of a minimum of 2 days to vote.
  2. A proposal shall pass by a simple majority and when quorum has been reached.
  1. Other thresholds or vote counting methods may be created by amending this Charter through a simple majority vote.
  1. A Governance Sub-Committee or Team may be formed to help members write proposals and guide them through the governance process.
  2. Initial proposal forum is the Proposal sections of the Origami platform.
  1. This may be changed by the Governance Committee without a Charter amendment.
  1. Proposals shall be posted in the DAO’s communication platform and may be signaled with emoji votes — which are non-binding — to create feedback-only before they are posted for a binding vote.
  1. Initial communication platform is the DAO’s Discord server.

  1. Quorum

  1. Quorum is calculated from the number of $KOMM tokens in “circulation among DAO Members”.
  1. “Circulation among DAO Members” is defined as the number of governance tokens held in DAO Member Wallets and tokens delegated to such wallets.
  1. DAO Member Wallets are defined as one containing a valid DAO Membership NFT.
  1. Tokens excluded from voting and quorum calculations
  1. Tokens held by the Treasury do not carry any voting power and do not count towards quorum as these tokens may not be delegated.
  2. Tokens held by non-members that are not delegated to a Member in good standing are excluded from quorum calculations.
  3. Unminted tokens are not in circulation.
  1. The default quorum threshold is 10% of governance tokens in circulation.
  1. E.g. If we have 1,000 total tokens in circulation, then we’ll need 10%% of those tokens to vote to reach the quorum threshold.
  2. The DAO may change the default quorum threshold by vote.
  1. The Quorum threshold is defined by the total number of tokens that have voted in a proposal.
  1. The DAO may create different types of proposals that require a higher or lower quorum threshold through the existing proposal process.
  1. The Governance Committee is responsible for setting the process for proposals not explicitly defined in this Charter.
  2. If two consecutive proposals fail due to not meeting quorum, the quorum requirement is halved.
  1. Once a proposal passes, the previous quorum threshold is reinstated automatically.

  1. Delegation

  1. Delegates are DAO Members who have been delegated voting power from another party’s governance tokens through an on-chain transaction.
  1. Only DAO Members in good standing may be Delegates.
  2. Non-Members who hold the DAO’s governance token may only delegate their token votes to a DAO Member in good standing.
  1. Delegators
  1. The DAO allows DAO Members and other governance token holders to explicitly delegate their votes to any DAO member in good standing.
  1. Delegators and/or Delegates may remove the delegation at any time for any reason.
  1. No delegation of votes may be permanent.
  2. The Treasury Committee may regulate the maximum amount of time a token may be delegated before it requires re-delegation.
  1. Tokens held by the Treasury, the Committees, or Team wallets are not eligible for Delegation.

  1. Initial Token Distribution

  1. The DAO may establish multiple types of tokens by proposal, however only the $KOMM governance token will be used for voting on Proposals, including elections. Other tokens will not count towards quorum or votes.

  2. Initial Mint

  1. Upon the Effective Date of this Charter, the DAO will mint one billion (1,000,000,000) $KOMM governance tokens.
  1. These tokens currently have no value other than governance.
  2. Governance tokens will be restricted from being transferred out from member wallets unless this transfer restriction is lifted by DAO proposal.
  1. The DAO may mint more tokens beyond the initial {{token total}}  by proposal.

  1. Budget for Season 0 (Genesis).

  1. Season 0 Member Token Pool: Fifty million (50,000,000) $KOMM to allocate equally across all DAO members who sign the Charter by the Season 0 signing deadline (June 30, 2023).
  2. Origami Token Allocation: For services provided to the DAO, 5% tokens will be sent to joinorigami.eth over the period of 18 months from the end of the 6-month lockup period following the token launch.

  1. Season 0 (Genesis) Member Token Eligibility

  1. All DAO members who sign this Charter with their ERC-20 wallet address before the effective date will receive a portion of the Genesis Member token pool.
  2. Every Genesis Member will receive the same number of tokens from this pool.
  3. Any undistributed or uncollected tokens from this pool will be returned to the Treasury.

  1. Season 0 (Genesis) Contributor Token Eligibility

  1. A Genesis Contributor is someone nominated by other members due to their contributions to the DAO prior to the issuance of DAO tokens.

  1. Seasonal Budgets and Token Allocations

  1. All token allocations to committees, teams, projects, and individuals require a proposal to pass.
  2. All allocations are distributed on a Season-by-Season basis unless otherwise noted.

  1. Starter Pack

  2. Any new member who joins and onboards to the DAO after the Genesis event will receive a number of tokens.
  1. The Treasury Committee is responsible for setting and changing the number of DAO governance tokens in each Starter Pack, unless a Proposal setting the amount has been approved by the DAO.

  1. Building the DAO Future

  1. During our Genesis season we will measure health based on a set of parameters that are flexible to accommodate learning and incentive-based to encourage participation and propel Programs and growth.
  1. Health measurements will surely change as learning accelerates and membership expands.
  2. We cannot anticipate all problems, but the hope is that gaps in procedure will be filled through proposals.

  1. DAO Health

  1. Our goal is to align the DAO members to a shared set of objectives and encourage participation in the activities to achieve key results. The Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) are measurements we use to evaluate how the DAO is performing over time.
  2. The baseline KPIs include financials, member engagement, and proposals.
  3. The measurements will answer questions such as, how is the investment portfolio performing, what percentage of proposals are contributing to deal flow, and how many members are actively participating in voting.
  4. The indicators will be measured real-time, monthly or seasonally and will be accessible by all members.

  1. Knowledge Sharing

  1. To accelerate the world’s transition to equitable and just governance while supporting our network of world-class founders, we must document our stories and share them with the world.
  2. Members are encouraged to share Komm DAO stories via social media and conferences.

  1. Investment Module

  1. DAO may invest in certain investment funds as a limited partner, based on the prospectus such as a PPM or Form C of any such fund. Entering a limited partnership with a fund entity requires a DAO vote, even if the fund has the same management team as any prior fund.

  2. Fund Partnerships

  1. This Charter officially recognizes the Lecca Ventures as benefiting the DAO. The hiring or firing of Funds requires a proposal to pass. The DAO may work with multiple funds at any given time. DAO members may work for the Funds.
  1. Investing
  1. The DAO may invest in startups but is not a fund itself. The DAO may invest as a limited partner of funds that invest in early-stage startups. The DAO may also make direct or indirect investments by forming a new fund entity or by co-investing.
  2. If the DAO makes investments through other funds, these funds must be independently managed by the fund’s managers who may receive advice and input from DAO members, committees, or processes. The DAO and General Partners (GP’s) of these funds may exchange some or all of the GP carry for the DAO’s tokens.
  3. This Charter further proposes the fund management will receive a 2% management fee and 15% carry. When a token distribution event occurs, a carry of 15% will be taken out from the total token amount. The 15% carry may be used to compensate the management team and/or DAO members who contributed in the investment process.
  1. Future Funds
  1. The DAO intends to invest as a limited partner in future funds that benefit the DAO. Terms are to be negotiated by the Treasury Committee.

  1. Legal Agreement

  1. The DAO and its members agree to be governed by the laws of the United States.
  2. We agree to respect the confidentiality of the DAO.
  3. The DAO will indemnify its Permanent Committee members.

Member Signatures
